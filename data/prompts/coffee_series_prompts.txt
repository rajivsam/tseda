This series covers 35 years of coffee prices and has about 420 coffee prices reported over this period. The period ranges from 1990 to 2025. The approach to analysis was to use the PELT algorithm to segment this series based on distribution of prices. The RBF model was used for this purpose. If you are interested in the mean shifts of price behavior, then you should use the L1 or the L2 models. The number of price changes reported by the PELT algorithm is consistent with the number of density modes observed in the density plot of the prices over the period. The segmentation information is saved as an intermediate file. We then use the segmentation section of the tool to analyze the segment behaviors. If we have done the segmentation based on the RBF model, then the segments represent series of data points that behave similarly from a distribution perspective. In this analysis, we picked the second segment for analysis for illustrative purposes. The features in the segmentation view permit us to view a smoothed version of the of this series. We can then use the singular spectrum analysis tool to analyze the components of variation the second segment. Running SSA it is clear that the first three eigen values of the SSA decomposition accounnt for over 90 percent of the variation in the time series. The trend component explains most of the variation in the coffes prices for the second segment. The seasonal components explain or account for a small portion of the variation. The plots of the series for both smmothing and singular spectrum analysis are available in the tool. Another analyst could analyze section 1 and 3 to complete the analysis of this series.
