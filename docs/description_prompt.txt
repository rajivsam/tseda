1. Time series data is pervasive, business operations, IoT devices, personal consumer devices all produce digital data that we want to to collect and understand
for the purposes of improving a service or operation, monitor for legitmacy of use, understanding consumer behavior etc.
2. The data has a simple structure, a time stamp and the metric being collected.
3. This tool will consider the processing of such time series with a regular sampling frequency. This is a very commonly encountered because machines and devices often collect data on a regular schedule.
4. If the data is irregular, it can be resampled to have this regular structure.
5. Time series data can be utilized for various tasks, for example, to forecast future values, to verify and monitor use, we want to catch anomalies. To understand behavior, we may want to extract common temporal patterns, a task that goes by the name of motif discovery.
6. Before time series data is used to develop models for these tasks, it is imperative that the modeling team understand the characteristic features of the data, such as underlying seasonality, trend, number of distinct segments of the time series.
7. This understanding will help the modeling team make informed choices for the modeling process.
8. It also helps operations and planning personel to exploit this knowledge to develop better customer service and be more profitable.
9. tseda is a tool that will provide this understanding.
10. tseda has a three step workflow: (1) Global segmentation of the time series (2) Profiling of the individual segments (3) Consolidation of the global and segment information and logging this consolidated finding to a knowledge graph.
11. In tseda, it is possible to leverage the power of LLM's for research and content preparation.
12. When you get a new dataset, all you need to do is use a notebook to clean this dataset to produce your time series. You can use kmds, a knowledge management tool to capture the processing steps as you clean them. You can then use tseda to explore and document your new time series.
13. tseda also has a notebook interface. You can use functionality of tseda in a notebook.
14. The current examples are all business and economic data, examples of sensor data will follow in a subsequent iteration. We do have some synthetic datasets that provinde a preview of how tseda can be applied to understand and process sensor signals.
